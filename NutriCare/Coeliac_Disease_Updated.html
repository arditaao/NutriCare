Coeliac Disease

What is Coeliac Disease?

Coeliac disease is an autoimmune condition in which the body's immune system reacts abnormally to gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. Even small amounts of gluten can damage the small intestine, leading to various symptoms and potential long-term health complications.

  • Symptoms: Can include digestive issues, fatigue, anemia, and joint pain.
  • Complications: If untreated, it can lead to nutrient deficiencies, osteoporosis, and increased risk of other autoimmune disorders.
  • Diagnosis: Blood tests and a biopsy of the small intestine are commonly used for diagnosis.

Treatment & Management

The only effective treatment for coeliac disease is a strict lifelong gluten-free diet. Removing gluten allows the intestine to heal and prevents further damage.

  • Gluten-Free Diet: Avoid wheat, barley, rye, and foods that contain gluten.
  • Medical Check-Ups: Regular blood tests and monitoring of gut health are recommended.
  • Nutritional Support: Consulting a dietitian can help manage nutrient intake.

Living with coeliac disease requires careful dietary planning, but with the right support and lifestyle adjustments, individuals can lead a healthy life.
