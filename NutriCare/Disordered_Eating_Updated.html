Disordered Eating & Eating Disorders

Understanding Disordered Eating

Disordered eating refers to irregular eating behaviors that may not qualify as a diagnosed eating disorder but still impact physical and mental well-being. Unlike a formally diagnosed eating disorder, disordered eating patterns can appear in individuals of all body types and often involve an unhealthy focus on food, weight, and dieting.

  • Emotional Eating: Using food to cope with emotions rather than for hunger.
  • Compulsive Eating: Feeling out of control with food intake, often leading to guilt.
  • Anxiety Around Food: Avoiding social events due to fear of food choices.
  • Obsession with Food & Weight: Spending excessive time worrying about food intake and body image.

What is an Eating Disorder?

Eating disorders are serious mental health conditions that affect people of all backgrounds. They involve unhealthy relationships with food and body image and can have severe physical consequences.

  • Anorexia Nervosa: Characterized by extreme food restriction, weight loss, and an intense fear of gaining weight.
  • Bulimia Nervosa: Involves binge eating followed by purging behaviors such as vomiting or excessive exercise.
  • Binge Eating Disorder: Regular episodes of eating large amounts of food without purging.
  • Orthorexia: An obsession with healthy eating that leads to severe food restrictions.

If you or someone you know is struggling with disordered eating or an eating disorder, seeking help from a medical professional is crucial. Recovery is possible with the right support and guidance.
